Steak

Description

A delicious steak is tender and juicy, with a perfectly seared crust that adds a satisfying crunch. The inside is cooked to your preferred doneness, from a soft, pink medium-rare to a more well-done, hearty bite. However, if you like your steaks well-done, I take pity on your soul.

The rich, savory flavors of the beef are enhanced by a touch of seasoningâ€”maybe a sprinkle of salt, cracked black pepper, or a hint of garlic. It might be finished off with a dollop of butter that melts into the meat, adding an extra layer of richness. Every bite is an indulgence in flavor and texture.

Ingredients

  • 16 oz ribeye steak
  • 1 tbsp kosher salt
  • 1.5 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp avocado oil
  • 2 tbsp butter

Steps

  1. Salt both sides of steak with 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt per steak. Place steak in an airtight container and refrigerate 48 hours.
  2. Remove steak from the refrigerator about 30 minutes prior to cooking. Sprinkle both sides of steak with black pepper.
  3. Heat a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat until very hot. Add avocado oil; heat until oil shimmers.
  4. Carefully place steak in skillet. Cook until brown and hard-seared on one side, 4 to 5 minutes.
  5. Flip steak; top with 1 teaspoon butter. Cook until desired doneness, or 3 to 4 minutes more. Keep cooked steak in a 170 degrees F ( 77 degrees C) oven until ready to serve.
